## BAGUETTE MODIFICATION

From the standard recipe, adjust as follows for baguettes:

**Ingredients (makes 3-4 baguettes):**
- 150g active starter
- 400g warm water (~67% hydration — lower than standard)
- 600g bread flour
- 12g salt

**Key changes:**
- Lower hydration (~67%) for easier shaping
- More stretch-and-folds: 4–6 sets (vs 3–4) for gluten strength
- Divide into 3-4 pieces, pre-shape into loose cylinders, rest 20-30 min
- Final shape: flatten, fold top third down, fold bottom up, roll and taper ends to 12-14 inches
- Proof seam-side up in a floured couche or tea towel pleats
- Bake at 475°F with steam (add a cup of boiling water to a pan in the oven) — no Dutch oven needed
- 15 min with steam, then 15-20 min at 450°F until deep golden
- Cool 30 min minimum; baguettes crisp as they cool

## SOURDOUGH CINNAMON ROLLS (with discard)

**Dough (yields ~12 rolls):**
- 500g all-purpose flour
- 240g sourdough discard (room temp)
- 180ml warm milk
- 50g granulated sugar
- 57g unsalted butter, melted
- 2 large eggs (room temp)
- 2¼ tsp active dry yeast (optional — see no-yeast version below)
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp vanilla extract

**Filling:**
- 113g unsalted butter, softened
- 200g brown sugar
- 2 tbsp cinnamon
- Pinch of salt

**Cream cheese icing:**
- 113g cream cheese, softened
- 57g unsalted butter, softened
- 180g powdered sugar
- 2 tbsp heavy cream
- 1 tsp vanilla
- Pinch of salt

**With yeast:** Activate yeast in warm milk + sugar (5-10 min until foamy). Mix wet ingredients, add flour. Knead 5-8 min. First rise 45-60 min until doubled. Roll 16x12 in, spread filling, roll and slice. Second rise 30-45 min. Bake 350°F for 20-25 min. Frost warm.

**WITHOUT yeast (true sourdough):**
- Use 200-240g ACTIVE starter (peaked and bubbly), not discard
- Reduce milk to ½ cup initially
- Mix dough, bulk ferment overnight 8-12 hours at room temp
- Shape in the morning, second rise 1-2 hours, bake 350°F 20-25 min
- Slightly denser but more flavor; overnight in fridge after shaping works great

## SOURDOUGH DISCARD CUPCAKES

Ultra-moist, tender cupcakes with subtle tang. Baking powder/soda handles rise.

**Batter (12 cupcakes):**
- 150g sourdough discard (unfed, room temp)
- 150g unsalted butter, melted (or vegetable oil)
- 3 large eggs (room temp)
- 100g whole milk
- 10g vanilla extract
- 200g all-purpose flour
- 200g granulated sugar
- 7g baking powder
- 4g baking soda
- 4g salt

**Method:** Whisk wet ingredients (including discard) until smooth — discard may look clumpy at first, keep whisking. Mix dry ingredients separately. Combine just until no dry streaks. Fill liners ¾ full. Bake 350°F 16-20 min until toothpick clean.

**Buttercream:**
- 180g butter softened, beat until creamy
- 375g powdered sugar + 2-3 tbsp milk + 1 tsp vanilla + pinch of salt

**Chocolate version:** Swap ¼–⅓ cup flour for cocoa powder, add ½ cup hot coffee.

## SPROUTED WHOLE WHEAT ADJUSTMENT (standard recipe)

- **Hydration:** Start with 375-400g water (vs 350g). Sprouted flour varies in absorption.
- **Fermentation:** Faster due to enzyme activity — check bulk at 6-10 hours not 8-12.
- **Folds:** Same 3-4 sets, but handle gently.
- **Autolyse:** Shorten to 20-30 min (less enzyme breakdown).
- **Optional:** Add 10-20g vital wheat gluten to improve rise.
- **Crumb:** Denser, heartier, nuttier. Crust may brown faster — tent with foil if needed.
- **Beginner tip:** Start with 50-75% sprouted + 25-50% bread flour for better rise.

## EINKORN FLOUR ADJUSTMENT (standard recipe)

Einkorn = ancient wheat, lower gluten, nutty flavor, faster fermentation, stickier dough.

- **Hydration:** Lower to 300-350g (start with 300g) — einkorn needs less water.
- **Starter:** Can reduce to 75-80g if starter is very active (ferments fast).
- **Folds:** Reduce to 3-4 gentle sets. Less benefit from aggressive gluten building.
- **Autolyse:** 20-30 min max.
- **Bulk ferment:** 6-10 hours. Check early — overproofing is the main risk.
- **Proof:** Shorter: 45-90 min. Consider baking in a loaf pan for better height.
- **Optional:** Add 10-20g vital wheat gluten for structure.
- **Beginner tip:** Start with 50-75% einkorn + 25-50% bread flour.

## TROUBLESHOOTING

### Too much water in the dough (high hydration)
- Don't add flour after the folds have started — it disrupts fermentation and creates uneven texture.
- Instead: continue stretch-and-folds with wet hands. The dough should tighten.
- Use a bench scraper to handle. Use rice flour in the proofing basket.
- Bake at 475°F for better oven spring with wet doughs.
- Next time: add water gradually during mixing, not all at once.

### Gummy/snotty starter when dissolving in water
- Stir or whisk vigorously for 1-2 minutes. Let sit 10-15 min to soften clumps.
- Usually normal: gluten/starch not dissolving, or slightly over-fermented texture.
- For "ropiness" (stringy like mucus): discard most, feed with unbleached all-purpose flour for a few cycles to reset bacteria.
- Clean the jar between feedings to avoid buildup.
- Won't ruin the bread — proceed with the dough and it smooths out during folds.

### Overfeeding the starter
- Adding too much flour and water dilutes the yeast/bacteria → sluggish activity.
- Wait up to 24 hours for it to catch up before using.
- Or: discard most, keep 20-50g, feed normally. Repeat until bubbly and doubling reliably.
- Next time: stick to 1:1:1 ratio by weight.

### Dense or flat loaf
- Starter wasn't active enough — feed again and wait for full bubble activity.
- Overproofed — check dough earlier next time (poke test).
- Underproofed — give more time.
- Too cool in the kitchen — find a warmer spot or use a slightly longer fermentation.

## STORAGE

### Fresh-baked loaf
- Don't bag a warm loaf — trapped steam = soggy crust and faster mold.
- Cool completely on a wire rack (at least 1-2 hours, ideally more).
- Room temp: store in a paper bag, linen bag, or bread box (not plastic) for 3-5 days.
- **Never refrigerate** — it dries out bread rapidly.
- **Freeze:** Slice first, wrap tightly in plastic or beeswax wrap, freeze up to 3 months. Thaw at room temp or toast from frozen.

## 12 HOURS vs 24 HOURS FERMENTATION COMPARISON

| | 12 Hours | 24 Hours |
|---|---|---|
| **Flavor** | Milder, balanced, fresh | Deeper, tangier, more complex |
| **Crumb** | Open, good holes, light | Can be more tender or dense if over-fermented |
| **Rise** | 50-100% volume, strong structure | Risk of over-proofing at room temp |
| **Crust** | Light golden | Darker, more caramelized |
| **Ease** | More forgiving, great for beginners | Needs monitoring, cooler temps recommended |

**At room temperature:** 12 hours is usually the sweet spot. 24 hours risks over-fermentation.

**For a 24-hour result without the risk:** Do a "cold bulk" — mix and fold in the evening, then refrigerate overnight through the next day. Slower fermentation = complex flavor, no sourness spike, more flexibility.

**Judge by the dough, not the clock:**
- 50-100% volume increase ✓
- Bubbles on surface and sides ✓
- Jiggles when shaken ✓
- Slow spring-back on poke test ✓
- Pleasant tangy smell (not vinegary) ✓
